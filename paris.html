Dreaming of Paris

Paris is a city that shows up in art, movies, and history books, but I’ve never seen it in person. When I picture Paris, I think of narrow streets, balconies full of plants, warm light from cafes, and the sound of footsteps on old stone.

Landmarks

The Eiffel Tower, the Louvre, Notre-Dame, and the Arc de Triomphe are classic spots. I’d love to see them, but also find lesser-known corners of the city.

Cafes & Bakeries

Coffee with a view of the street, fresh baguettes, croissants, and desserts lined up behind glass — spending a quiet morning in a Parisian cafe is high on my list.

Neighborhood Walks

From Montmartre to the Latin Quarter, I want to wander through neighborhoods to get a sense of how people live, not just where tourists go.

Longer-Term Travel Notes

I don’t have firm plans to visit Paris yet, but I’m slowly gathering ideas. As I learn more about transportation, seasons, and neighborhoods, I’ll add packing lists, walking routes, and sample itineraries here.

For now, this section is a place for rough notes, half-formed plans, and inspiration — enough text to keep the page full and readable while I keep researching.
